01 — Senior Design

PLA Recycler

Fusion 360 ANSYS Fluent MATLAB Arduino Mega Thermal Systems

Fused deposition modeling generates significant plastic waste through failed prints and single-use prototypes. This senior design project tackled that directly — a desktop PLA recycling system capable of processing waste filament back into usable feedstock.

I owned the melting and extrusion subsystem end to end. The extrusion chamber was machined from aluminum for thermal mass and mounted to an 80/20 frame. Thermal isolation was handled with PTFE spacers at frame contacts and ceramic fiber insulation elsewhere. Temperature control runs on an Arduino Mega with thermistor feedback driving cartridge heaters — giving closed-loop control over the melt zone.

I designed the chamber geometry in Fusion 360, validated flow behavior and thermal gradients with ANSYS Fluent CFD simulations, and used MATLAB for initial heat transfer and throughput calculations before committing to hardware.

Testing in progress — performance metrics to be added shortly.

03 — Class Project

NDE of Additive Manufactured Parts

MATLAB Ultrasonic NDT X-ray CT AM Characterization
Add CT scan images · MATLAB plots

A semester-long series of investigations applying nondestructive evaluation techniques to additively manufactured components — a timely problem given AM's rapid adoption in structural applications where part qualification remains a challenge.

Ultrasonic characterization — used contact and immersion transducers to measure mechanical properties of AM parts, extracting elastic moduli from wave speed and attenuation data.

Porosity via X-ray CT — processed volumetric scan data in MATLAB to quantify void content in AM specimens. Porosity is a primary failure driver in AM, and non-destructive quantification is directly relevant to aerospace and automotive qualification.

Fresnel lens evaluation — investigated AM Fresnel lenses as ultrasonic focusing elements. Lenses in PLA, PETG, and CF variants were printed and analyzed for focal gain and spot size in MATLAB. Results showed scattering rather than focusing, traced to dimensional inaccuracies inherent to FDM at required feature scales — a result that meaningfully bounds where FDM acoustic optics are and aren't viable.
